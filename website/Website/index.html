What are the Chars?

The chars are the collection of islands and bars created by the erosion and constant flooding around the areas near the rivers Brahmaputra, Ganges and Meghna. These areas are difficult to access where around 600.000 people live under extreme conditions.

The chars conform the most impoverished areas of the country where lots of help is needed. Many ONGs and volunteer groups are working hardly to improve the life conditions of the communities being one the Enactus group with the Charge Project.

As we can see, although the Char region does not have complete access to basic services, from the year 2000 there has been a visible improvement. The joint work of international and national NGO's and the government of Bangladesh has improved the quality of life for the communities living along the Char region.

The Charge Project

What have we achieved so far?

2 boats
4 solar panels per boat
1 storage battery per boat
16 powerbanks per boat
100 users per boat
Daily Earnings: 5p per user

The Charge Project is a team of students from the University of Southampton who work along with the Gana Unnayan Kendra (GUK), one of Bangladesh largest NGOs to provide sources of renewable energy to the communities located along the Char region. With the implementation of a fleet of boats with solar panels in their roofs and that go around the rivers in the area, this initiative aims to distribute battery packs to the communities to provide power lighting and mobile phones.
